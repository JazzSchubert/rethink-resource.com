Welcome!

Upcycling is the process of changing the application of a material from low value, such as incineration or bio-gas, to high value, such as food, cosmetics, fashion, etc.

Many industrial Side Streams today are Waste Streams, because no better application has been found and the material goes to waste. However, with the trends towards product diversification, responsible consumption and digitalisation, new applications appear every day. Start-ups and big players from all industries are pushing the boundaries of our perception of waste, resources and value.

Through this new mindset emerge products that are not only more sustainable than their counterparts, but more profitable as well.

Secondary Single-Colour Anodised Aluminium:

The pioneer pocket knife with scales made of recycled Nespresso capsules. To make the Alox scales, used Nespresso capsules are molten down and then cast. This saves enormous amounts of energy required for the production of virgin aluminium

The first knife was created in 2016, to celebrate the 25th anniversary of Nespresso’s recycling program. Thanks to the success of this knife, Victorinox and Nespresso have continued the partnership in 2017 and 2018.

www.victorinox.com

Secondary PET as Form-Giving Design Element:

PET Lamp believe in reuse as the counterpoint to recycling. PET Lamp is a project that mixes the reuse of PET plastic bottles with selected traditional weaving techniques from different corners of the world in order to create unique handmade lampshades.

They proudly created the magic moment when PET plastic stripes and natural fibres meet and perfectly fit together in order to turn into a long-lasting item. Each lampshade is unique and is offered as individual lamps as well as big installations that enhance the infinite variety of pieces.

petlamp.org

Secondary Protein and Fibre from Spent Grain:

ReGrained rescues the nutritious grain created every time that beer is brewed, and upcycles it into SuperGrain+ flour using their patent-pending tech.

Brewing beer processes the sugar out of the grain. This gives optimal access to protein, fiber, and a whole bunch of micronutrients that they upcycle into SuperGrain+. It not only out-performs nearly every other grain flour in nutrients like protein and fiber, but also packs incredible flavor and environmental regenerative impact.

www.regrained.com

Spent Grain as Potato-Replacer:

Fresh Spent Grain from the brewhouse and vitamin-rich yeast from the fermenting cellar. Brauerei Locher from Appenzell is upcycling their side streams into an innovative Snäck.

The by-products from the brewery are usefully refined into a taste-intensive and authentic top-quality product. With many vegetable proteins, minerals and dietary fibers - a high-quality Appenzeller Snäck with less fat.

www.tschipps.ch

Secondary Plastics and Rubber:

Adidas and Parley collaborate to prevent plastic entering our oceans and transform it into high performance sportswear.

Spinning the problem into a solution. The threat into a thread. They are driving eco innovation around materials, products and new ways of using them. New Methods. New Mindsets. New Future.

www.adidas.ch

Secondary Single-Colour Respun Non-Dyed Fibre:

The people at Pure Waste know, that everything we do has an impact on something, somewhere. They know that sustainability and quality go together. They believe that fashion and ecological thinking should go together too. “Pure Waste” is their promise to use only materials that would otherwise go to waste.

They also wish to challenge the whole perception of waste. Pure Waste is recycling upgraded. Their vision is to recreate the fashion industry, and lead it into a future of sustainability. They want to industrialise textile upcycling globally as a common practice.

www.purewaste.org

Coffee Grounds as Food-Safe Structural Component:

The starting point for Kaffeform UG was the simple question: What happens to all the coffee grounds after we have enjoyed our Espresso, Cappuccino or Americano? Is there no way to re-use this good and natural resource?

Characteristic features of Kaffeeform are the marbled, wood-like surface, the smell of coffee, the light weight, the long durability and washability (dishwasher safe).

www.kaffeeform.com

Unsold Bread as Sustainable Malt-Replacer:

We are used to having a large selection of freshly baked bread at any time of the day. That's why around one in four loaves remains unsold. The people at Damn Good Food & Beverages AG thought: The long way from the seed to the crispy loaf should not have been in vain. So they chopped up the bread to crumbs and made their great bread beer out of it.

Besides bread crumbs, Damn Good Bread Beer is brewed with fresh spring water, yeast, hops and barley malt. This produces the amber and tasty specialty beer.

www.breadbeer.ch

High-Fibre Side Streams as Structural Component & Binder:

The E6PR™ (Eco Six Pack Ring) is the first ecofriendly six pack ring made from by-product waste and other compostable materials, designed to replace the plastic rings, which are truly damaging to our environment.

When disposed of properly, the E6PR™ finds its way to a compostable facility, where it will degrade in days, and when, unfortunately, left out in open land or a water system, it will degrade within a week. It is made from compostable organic materials that do not cause harm to wildlife in case of ingestion.​

www.e6pr.com

Agricultural Side Stream as Vegan Leather Replacement:

Piñatex® is an innovative natural textile made from pineapple leaf ﬁbre. The raw material that forms the base of Piñatex® is a by-product of the pineapple harvest. The leaves are the byproduct of existing agriculture, and their use creates an additional income stream for farming communities.

Piñatex® is a non-woven textile, developed for use as a low-impact alternative to leather and PVC/PU materials. It has passed several ISO textile testing standards and is available in six colours.

www.ananas-anam.com

Secondary Fishing Net Nylon Yarn:

An elegant design solution for the floor and a socially responsible design solution for a fragile economy. Net Effect's project Net-Works provides a source of income for small fishing villages in the Philippines while cleaning up their beaches and waters of discarded fishing nets that threaten their livelihood.

Discarded fishing nets are collected and sold to their yarn supplier and partner, Aquafil. Since 2011 they’ve been re-purposing waste nylon from discarded fishing nets and other sources, including yarn reclaimed through their own ReEntry® program, to provide recycled content nylon for Interface carpet tiles.

www.interface.com

Secondary Polyester Fibre for Garments:

Patagonia began making recycled polyester from plastic soda bottles in 1993–the first outdoor clothing manufacturer to transform trash into fleece. It was a positive step toward a more sustainable system – one that uses fewer resources, discards less and better protects people’s health.

Today, they recycle used soda bottles, unusable manufacturing waste and worn-out garments (including their own) into polyester fibers to produce clothing. And they offer recycled polyester in a lot more garments, including Capilene® baselayers, shell jackets, board shorts and fleece.

www.patagonia.com

Secondary Protein Concentrate and Lipids:

Insects are some of nature's most efficient converters of low grade organic materials to concentrated protein and lipids. In most cultures, reared insects are a highly regarded specialty.

Protix is the leading insect ingredient company with a broad range of ingredients made from the Black Soldier Fly, Mealworm, Cricket and Locust. Protix is dedicated to delivering sustainable, circular and natural proteins and ingredients from insects for the production of high quality food and animal feed.

protix.eu

Soy Milk Side Stream as Cosmetic Ingredient:

Rene Furterer's Okara Active Light Shampoo uses natural Okara extract as a main ingredient. The amino acids restructure and repair damaged hair and provide incredible brilliance.

In Japan, Korea and China, Okara is part of the traditional cuisines. The Western culture, lacking traditional application in food, has downcycled Okara as feed for livestock. Upcycling this high-quality ingredient in premium cosmetics is not only more sustainable, but also promotes the ingredient for other high-value applications.

www.renefurterer.com

High-Fibre Side Streams as Insulation Material:

The company Ecovative Design developed a compound called Greensulate. It uses mycelium to bind natural insulating materials such as rice hulls or cotton husks—whatever agricultural byproducts are available in the area.

Currently there are two primary variants available, with different compressive strength, flexure strength and flame retardance. They also produce a mycellium-based textile that is used as leather replacement

ecovativedesign.com

Secondary Rubber as Shoe Soles:

Timberland Tires are the first premium automotive tires purposely created to be recycled into footwear outsoles at the end of their life on the road. When the tread wears out, the tire lives on, one day recycled into Timberland® shoes.

Rather than being used for tire-derived fuel or ending up in a landfill, Timberland Tires will be given a second life within the outsole of a boot or shoe. Fewer tires end up in landfills; less virgin rubber gets used in footwear.

www.timberlandtires.com

Secondary Glass Compound as Marble Replacement:

IceStone manufacture upcycled glass surfaces for kitchen countertops, bathroom vanities and desk tops in their state-of-the-art facility in the historic Brooklyn Navy Yard. They use just three core ingredients: 100% recycled glass, Portland cement, and non-toxic pigments.

They also created PaperStone, a heavy-duty solid surface known for its performance, warm touch, contemporary appearance and environmental sustainability. Its non-porous surface is made from 100% post-consumer recycled paper and a proprietary, petroleum-free resin.

icestoneusa.com

Agrigultural Side Stream for Packaging Synergy:

For its new tomato product, Pure Hothouse Foods decided to use solid board enriched with tomato plant fibres, which is uniquely produced by Solidus Solutions, manufacturer of solid board and solid board packaging.

Every hectare of tomato plants can produce 100,000 tomato boxes (trays of 6kg), which is exactly the amount of packaging needed for the 600 tonnes of tomatoes that are grown on the same hectare. This means that this year's crop can provide next year's packaging - a perfect example of maximum use.

solidus-solutions.com

sealandgear.com

Secondary Fibre for Disposable Tableware and Packaging:

BIO-LUTIONS offers packaging solutions and disposable tableware made of up-cycled agricultural excess. This raw material is converted through a mechanical process into self-binding Micro- and Nanofibrillated Natural Fibres.

BIO-LUTIONS uses no chemical additives and does not have to go through the energy-intensive process of cellulose extraction. This way, they avoid chemical contamination and excessive use of water, both common to cellulose production. Their technology makes it possible to use even the shortest fibres from most agricultural excess worldwide.

www.bio-lutions.com

Secondary Cotton & Wood Fibre:

The need for clothing will have doubled by the year 2025. This amount of clothing signals a major burden for our environment. Refibra™ fibers, the new TENCEL™ fibers, are made from pulp that contains cotton scraps left over from cutting operations and wood.

Lenzing is the first manufacturer to offer cellulose fibers featuring upcycled material on a commercial scale. With their technology, Lenzing pioneer the circular economy for textiles, reducing the need to extract additional raw materials.

www.lenzing-fibres.com

The people at ECOJAZZ turn pollution into high-performance acoustic absorbers and turn the waste problem into a solution. ECHOJAZZ upcyles between 47-72 PET bottles from landfills and the Pacific per square meter of product.

Their main product EchoPanel is more ecological, more robust, lighter and more flexible than any comparable product.

Unsold Bread as Carbohydrate for Beer:

The people at Toast Ale Ltd are on a mission to prevent food waste. Their delicious beer is brewed with unsold loaves from bakeries and unused crusts from sandwich makers.

They have taken secondary sourcing one step further and replaced all their barley malt with unsold bread. The only other ingredients are hops, yeast and water.

www.toastale.com

Fruit Processing Side Streams as Cosmetics Ingredient:

Fruu are a small cosmetics company from the UK. Their products are largely made of ingredients extracted and derived from more than 15 different types of nutrient bursting fruits. 100% bio-degradable.

Most of the ingredients are produced as a by-product of processed fruit waste, which generates vital extra revenue for small fruit farmers. All products are made in small batches in our UK workshop to make sure they are as fresh as possible.

fruuurskin.com

Damaged Fire Hose as Sheet Material:

In 2005 Elvis & Kresse had a chance encounter with the London Fire Brigade. When they learned that London's damaged decommissioned hoses were headed to landfill they mounted a rescue. They established Elvis & Kresse to save it.

For over a decade none of London's fire-hose has gone to landfill and over 170 tons of material have been reclaimed. The value created by their upcycling innovation is so high that they decided to donate 50% of the profits from their firehose range to The Fire Fighters Charity.

www.elvisandkresse.com
